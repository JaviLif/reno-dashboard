Method & assumptions

Design heat load = Q_fabric + Q_ventilation + Q_infiltration, multiplied by reheat allowance.

Fabric: U = 1/(Rc + R_surface) where R_surface = 0.17 walls/doors, 0.14 roof, 0.34 floor over crawlspace. Total = Σ(UA · b) × ΔT × (1 + bridges%).

Air: 0.34 × q_v × (1 − η) × ΔT for ventilation + 0.34 × q_inf × ΔT for infiltration. 0.34 = volumetric heat capacity of air in Wh/m³K.

Starting values (energy label): walls Rc 1.69 (cavity filled) · roof Rc 0.35 (uninsulated) · floor Rc 0.15 · natural ventilation · plastic frames 2000/2010.

Heat pump class = output at design outdoor temp, not the A7/W35 nameplate. DHW sized separately. UFH delivers ~60–70 W/m² at 35°C flow.

What CPI has actually done

CBS · consumentenprijsindex, alle huishoudens
10-yr average ≈ 3.0%

2015–2025 annual inflation, %: 0.6 · 0.3 · 1.4 · 1.7 · 2.6 · 1.3 · 2.7 · 10.0 · 3.8 · 3.3 · 3.3. December 2025 came in at 2.8%; January 2026 at 2.4%.

Realised CPI has averaged about 3.0%

The ten-year average is roughly 3.0% — and that is after a decade that included years at 0.3%. The model opens at 2.5%, which is below what has actually happened. The ECB targets 2.0%; the Netherlands has not averaged 2.0% since 2015.

Raising CPI from 2.5% to 3.0% moves roughly €2,300 from Javi to Rani over five years, and considerably more over ten. Drag the CPI slider and watch it. The direction is always the same: higher inflation favours the larger contributor.

2022 is the reason to read the small print

CPI printed 10.0% in 2022. On Rani's €218,000 that is €21,800 of indexation in a single year — from one number, published once.

CBS later switched to a better method for measuring energy prices (June 2023). Rabobank's reconstruction puts 2022 nearer 7.3% under the new method. CBS deliberately did not revise the published figures — precisely because CPI is used to index contracts, and revising it would retroactively rewrite them.

So: indexing on CPI means accepting whatever CBS printed at the time, methodological artefacts included. That is arguably the point — it is certain, public, and nobody can renegotiate it after the fact. But it is a choice, not a neutral default. Ask Eva whether the afgeleide CPI (which strips out changes in BTW, accijns and other indirect taxes) suits you better.

CBS rebased the index this year — write the clause as a ratio

From 2026 the CPI moved from 2015=100 to 2025=100, with a new classification of goods. Published inflation figures were not revised.

This is exactly why the deed must not name a fixed base year. Write it as a ratio, so any future rebasing cancels out:

vergoeding = inbreng × (CPImonth of sale ÷ CPImonth of contribution)

Sources: CBS — inflation 3.8% (2023), 3.3% (2024), 3.3% (2025), 10.0% (2022); rebasing 2015=100 → 2025=100 from 2026. Rabobank inflation monitor — 2022 reconstruction at 7.3%. Wettelijke rente 4% since 1 Jan 2026 (Staatsblad 2025, 438). HR 10 May 2019, ECLI:NL:HR:2019:707 — art. 1:87 BW does not apply to cohabitants.

Assumptions: annuity mortgage, monthly compounding; lump sum recalculates the annuity over the remaining term, same end date; no transaction costs on sale; CPI and house growth applied as constant annual rates. The erfpacht canon revision of March 2029 is not modelled and will weigh on value inside a five-year window.
